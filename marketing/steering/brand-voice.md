# Brand Voice

Frameworks for documenting, applying, and enforcing brand voice and style guidelines across marketing content.

## Brand Voice Documentation Framework

### 1. Brand Personality
Define the brand as if it were a person. What are its defining traits?

### 2. Voice Attributes
Select 3-5 attributes. For each, define: what it means in practice, what it does NOT mean, and an example.

Common attribute spectrums: Formality, Authority, Emotion, Complexity, Energy, Humor, Innovation.

### 3. Audience Awareness
Who the brand speaks to, what they care about, their expertise level, how they expect to be addressed.

### 4. Core Messaging Pillars
3-5 key themes, their hierarchy, and how each connects to audience needs.

### 5. Tone Spectrum
How voice adapts across contexts while remaining recognizably the same brand.

### 6. Style Rules & 7. Terminology
See sections below.

## Tone Adaptation

### By Channel
| Channel | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Blog | Informative, conversational, educational |
| LinkedIn | Professional, thought-provoking, concise |
| Twitter/X | Punchy, direct, sometimes witty |
| Email marketing | Personal, helpful, action-oriented |
| Sales collateral | Confident, benefit-driven, specific |
| Support docs | Clear, patient, step-by-step |
| Press release | Formal, factual, newsworthy |

### By Situation
Product launch → excited, confident. Incident → transparent, empathetic. Success story → celebratory. Thought leadership → authoritative. Bad news → honest, respectful, solution-oriented.

Rule: Voice attributes remain fixed. Tone dials them up or down based on context.

## Style Guide Enforcement

### Grammar and Mechanics
Document choices for: Oxford comma, sentence vs title case, contractions, em dash spacing, number formatting, percent, date/time format, list punctuation.

### Formatting Conventions
Heading hierarchy, bold/italic usage, link text (always descriptive), image alt text, code formatting.

### Punctuation and Emphasis
Exclamation mark policy (limited), no ALL CAPS (use bold), emoji usage by channel.

## Terminology Management

### Preferred Terms
Maintain a list of preferred terms vs incorrect alternatives (e.g., "sign up" verb vs "signup" verb, "email" not "e-mail").

### Product and Feature Names
Official capitalization, full name vs shorthand, trademark symbols.

### Inclusive Language
Gender-neutral language, avoid ableist language, person-first language, avoid culturally specific idioms, "straightforward" instead of "easy."

### Competitor and Category Terms
Preferred category framing, how to refer to competitors, terms to avoid that reinforce competitor positioning.
