# Campaign Plan Workflow

Generate a comprehensive marketing campaign brief.

## When to Use
When the user wants to plan, design, or build a marketing campaign.

## Inputs

1. **Campaign goal** (required): drive signups, increase awareness, launch product, generate leads, re-engage users
2. **Target audience** (required): demographics, roles, industries, pain points, buying stage
3. **Timeline** (required): duration and fixed dates
4. **Budget range** (optional): if not provided, generate channel-agnostic plan
5. **Additional context** (optional): product, differentiators, previous campaign learnings, brand guidelines, geographic focus

## Output Structure

1. **Campaign Overview**: Name suggestion, one-sentence summary, primary measurable objective, secondary objectives
2. **Target Audience**: Primary/secondary segments, pain points, where they spend time, buying stage
3. **Key Messages**: Core message, 3-4 supporting messages, proof points, differentiation
4. **Channel Strategy**: Per channel — why it fits, content format, effort level, budget allocation. Consider owned/earned/paid.
5. **Content Calendar**: Week-by-week table with content piece, channel, milestones, dependencies
6. **Content Pieces Needed**: Asset list with type, description, priority, timeline
7. **Success Metrics**: Primary KPI with target, 3-5 secondary KPIs, tracking method, reporting cadence
8. **Budget Allocation** (if provided): By channel, production vs distribution, contingency
9. **Risks and Mitigations**: 2-3 risks with mitigation strategies
10. **Next Steps**: Immediate actions, approvals needed, key decision points

See **campaign-planning** steering for detailed frameworks.

## Follow Up
Offer to: dive deeper into any section, draft specific content pieces, create competitive analysis, or adjust for different budget/timeline.
