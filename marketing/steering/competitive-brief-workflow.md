# Competitive Brief Workflow (Marketing)

Research competitors and generate a positioning and messaging comparison.

## When to Use
When the user wants a competitive analysis, competitor research, or market comparison from a marketing perspective.

## Inputs

1. **Competitor name(s)** (required)
2. **Your company/product context** (optional but recommended): what you sell, positioning, differentiators
3. **Focus areas** (optional): messaging/positioning, product comparison, content strategy, recent news, pricing, market presence

## Research Process

For each competitor via web search: website messaging, recent news (6 months), content strategy (blog, resources, social), review sites and comparisons, job postings for strategic signals.

## Output Structure

1. **Executive Summary**: 2-3 sentence landscape overview, biggest opportunity and threat
2. **Competitor Profiles**: Per competitor — overview, messaging analysis (tagline, value prop, themes, tone), product positioning, content strategy (frequency, types, SEO observations), strengths, weaknesses
3. **Messaging Comparison Matrix**: Table comparing tagline, target buyer, differentiator, tone, core value prop
4. **Content Gap Analysis**: Topics they cover that you don't, formats to adopt, keyword opportunities
5. **Opportunities**: Positioning gaps, unclaimed messaging angles, underserved segments, channel opportunities
6. **Threats**: Where competitors are strong, trends favoring their positioning, recent moves
7. **Recommended Actions**: 3-5 specific actions — quick wins and strategic moves

Note the date of research for freshness context.

## Follow Up
Offer to: create sales battlecard, draft messaging exploiting gaps, dive deeper on specific competitor, or set up monitoring plan.
