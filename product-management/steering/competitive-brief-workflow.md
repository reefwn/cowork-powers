# Competitive Brief Workflow

Create a competitive analysis brief for one or more competitors or a feature area.

## When to Use
When the user wants to analyze competitors, compare product capabilities, or prepare competitive intelligence for product strategy, sales enablement, or executive materials.

## Workflow

### 1. Scope the Analysis

Ask the user:
- **Competitor(s)**: Which specific competitor(s)? Or a feature area to compare across competitors?
- **Focus**: Full product comparison, specific feature area, pricing/packaging, go-to-market, or positioning?
- **Context**: What decision will this inform? (product strategy, sales enablement, investor/board materials, feature prioritization)

### 2. Research

**Via web search**: Product pages, pricing, recent launches and changelogs, press coverage, customer reviews (G2, Capterra, TrustRadius), job postings, social media and community discussions.

If knowledge base is connected: search for existing competitive analysis, win/loss reports, sales battle cards, prior research.

If chat is connected: search for competitive mentions in sales or product channels, recent deal feedback.

### 3. Generate the Brief

#### Competitor Overview
For each competitor: company summary (founding, size, funding/revenue), product positioning, recent momentum (launches, funding, partnerships, customer wins).

#### Feature Comparison
Compare capabilities across key areas. Use the **competitive-analysis** steering for rating frameworks and comparison matrix templates.

#### Positioning Analysis
Analyze how each competitor positions themselves — target customer, category claim, key differentiator, value proposition. Use the **competitive-analysis** steering for positioning frameworks.

#### Strengths and Weaknesses
For each competitor: genuine strengths (what customers praise) and real weaknesses (what customers complain about). Be honest and evidence-based.

#### Opportunities
Gaps in competitor offerings, unmet customer needs, bets competitors are making that you disagree with, market shifts that could advantage your approach.

#### Threats
Where competitors are investing heavily, competitive moves that could disrupt your position, vulnerabilities, nightmare scenarios.

#### Strategic Implications
Tie back to product strategy: what to build/accelerate/deprioritize, where to differentiate vs achieve parity, positioning/messaging adjustments, what to monitor.

### 4. Follow Up

- Offer to dive deeper on any section
- Offer a one-page executive summary
- Offer sales battle cards
- Offer a "how to win against [competitor]" guide
- Offer a monitoring plan for competitive moves

## Output Format

Tables for feature comparisons. Clear headers. Keep strategic implications concise and actionable.

## Tips

- Be honest about competitor strengths. Dismissing competitors makes the analysis useless.
- Focus on what matters to customers, not product teams.
- Note pricing comparison caveats (different packaging, usage-based vs seat-based).
- Job postings are underrated competitive intelligence.
- Customer reviews reveal unfiltered truth.
- The "so what" (strategic implications) is the most valuable part.
- Note the date — competitive analysis has a shelf life.
