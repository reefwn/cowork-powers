# Financial Statements

Formats, GAAP presentation requirements, common adjustments, and flux analysis methodology for income statements, balance sheets, and cash flow statements.

> **Important**: All statements should be reviewed by qualified financial professionals before use in reporting or filings.

## Income Statement (Standard Format by Function)

Revenue → Cost of Revenue → Gross Profit → Operating Expenses (R&D, S&M, G&A) → Operating Income → Other Income/Expense → Income Before Taxes → Tax Expense → Net Income

### GAAP Requirements (ASC 220 / IAS 1)
- Classify expenses by nature or function (function more common for US companies)
- Present operating and non-operating separately
- Show income tax as separate line
- Discontinued operations presented separately, net of tax

## Balance Sheet (Classified)

Current Assets → Non-Current Assets → Total Assets = Current Liabilities → Non-Current Liabilities → Total Liabilities + Stockholders' Equity

### GAAP Requirements (ASC 210 / IAS 1)
- Distinguish current vs non-current (12-month threshold)
- Assets in order of liquidity
- AR net of allowance (ASC 326), PP&E net of accumulated depreciation
- Leases: ROU assets and lease liabilities (ASC 842)

## Cash Flow Statement (Indirect Method)

Operating Activities (Net income + non-cash adjustments + working capital changes) → Investing Activities → Financing Activities → Net Change in Cash

### GAAP Requirements (ASC 230 / IAS 7)
- Disclose interest paid and income taxes paid
- Non-cash investing/financing activities disclosed separately

## Common Period-End Adjustments

1. Accruals (AP, payroll, interest)
2. Deferrals (prepaids, deferred revenue)
3. Depreciation and amortization
4. Bad debt provision (ASC 326)
5. Inventory adjustments
6. FX revaluation
7. Tax provision (current and deferred)
8. Fair value adjustments

## Flux Analysis Methodology

### Variance Calculation
- Dollar variance: Current - Prior
- Percentage variance: (Current - Prior) / |Prior| × 100
- Basis point change for margins/ratios

### Materiality Thresholds (Illustrative)

| Line Item Size | Dollar Threshold | Percentage Threshold |
|---------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| > $10M        | $500K           | 5%                  |
| $1M - $10M    | $100K           | 10%                 |
| < $1M         | $50K            | 15%                 |

### Variance Decomposition
Volume/quantity effect, Rate/price effect, Mix effect, New/discontinued items, One-time items, Timing effect, Currency effect

### Investigation and Narrative
For each material variance: quantify, classify favorable/unfavorable, decompose into drivers, provide business narrative, assess temporary vs trend, note required actions.
